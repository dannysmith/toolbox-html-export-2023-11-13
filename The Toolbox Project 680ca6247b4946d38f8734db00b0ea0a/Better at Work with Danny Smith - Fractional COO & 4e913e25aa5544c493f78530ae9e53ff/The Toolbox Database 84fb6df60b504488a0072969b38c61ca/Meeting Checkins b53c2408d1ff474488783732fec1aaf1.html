  • If you're with two people with the same first name, you can say a fourth word so it's clear who should go next.

If everyone sticks to three words we can check in very quickly, even with quite large groups.

Why's use this checkin?

  • It confirms everyone has their cameras and microphones working right at the start.
  • Everyone has to say something right at the start, and are more likely to feel comfortable speaking up later in the meeting.
  • We get a general sense of where everyone is at, both individually and collectively. So we can be present to it.
  • We start to establish openness and vulnerability by sharing a tiny bit about ourselves. But we also don't have to share too much.
  • Choosing the next person requires that we're present to the other people on the call, which helps brings our attention "into the room".
  • It's very quick.

Other checkins

The checkin above has some downsides ‚Äì mainly because it's so brief. If you want to do something different, here are a few ideas...

  • Gratitude - "one thing you're grateful for".
  • Appreciation - "one thing or person you appreciate, and why"
  • Learning - "one thing you've learned about yourself (or others) in the last month"
  • Getting to know each other - "something you love about your neighbourhood" or "your favourite meal this week" or "the country you most want to visit and why".
  • etc.

You could also just go round the room and answer "How are you doing and what have you been up to?" - much like you might with your friends.
